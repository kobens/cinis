World's most effective recycling of fly ash

Cinis Recycling is the first company in the world to develop a system than can recycle all components of fly ash from biomass-fuelled power plants. With this technology we have developed a sustainable alternative to landfilling and encapsulation as ways of dealing with the increasing mass of heavy metals ash.

Technology Cinis Recycling

Valuable fertilizer from a waste product

When biomass is removed from an ecosystem, plant nutrition such as nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium is removed from the soil. To be productive the soil will need fertilization.

The combustion of biomass to produce heat and electricity is considered CO2-neutral. Different plant components such as straw and wood are used. The biomass that fuels power plants is often waste products from farms and other industries.

The combustion of biomass leaves another waste product – a fly ash, containing heavy metals but also potassium. Due to the content of heavy metals the fly ash is typically disposed of in one of several ways. This means however that the useful potassium also goes to waste.

Separation of heavy metals and potassium

Our technology makes it possible to extract the valuable nutrient potassium from the fly ash and use it for fertilization.

The link in the potassium cycle

Cinis Recycling fundamentally recycles the potassium and reduces waste from biomass power plants.

Cinis Recycling has developed a technology that seperates heavy metals and potassium from fly ash. The pure potassium can be added to different fertilizers, the heavy metals are disposed of in Germany, and the last sludge ash can be used directly as a fertilizer.
